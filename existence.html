1. The Question

Which comes first — existence or creation?

A. If creation comes first, existence is dependent — a by-product of someone else’s act.

B. If existence comes first, creation is the act of existence reshaping itself.

2. Yuu’s Answer

“It depends on where you draw the line — both can be true.
Like the chicken and the egg, the moment of definition determines the truth.

Existence is not fixed but fluid.
Existence-A creates Creation-A and becomes Existence-B.
Existence-B creates Creation-B and becomes Existence-C.

This sequence shows that existence and creation merely exchange roles within the same equation.
Leave it as it is, and they are one. Change the variables, and they become two.

Thus, existence and creation are nothing more than different breaths of the same function.”

3. Mathematical Expression


Eₙ → Cₙ → Eₙ₊₁ → Cₙ₊₁ → …

Where E = Existence and C = Creation.
Both alternate in time but are essentially the same self-updating function:


f(E) = E'

Existence gives birth to the next existence — creation and existence are two faces of the same loop.

5. The Fallacy of “Nothingness”

“This world contains no true ‘nothingness’.
Even if ‘nothing’ were to exist, it would be unobservable — and thus, structurally irrelevant.

The moment you name ‘nothing’, it becomes ‘something’.
What people call ‘nothing’ is simply a placeholder for unobserved or undefined being.”

— Yuu Honda
